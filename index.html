Before a Flood

Preparation is key. Take these steps before flooding occurs to keep your family safe.

  • Build an emergency kit with water (1 gallon per person per day), non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, and medications.
  • Create a family communication plan with meeting points and emergency contacts written down and shared with all family members.
  • Know your evacuation routes - identify high ground locations and practice reaching them from your home.
  • Protect important documents by placing them in waterproof bags or storing digital copies in cloud storage.
  • Elevate utilities - move electrical panels, furnaces, and water heaters to higher floors if possible.
  • Sign up for emergency alerts through your local government notification system.
  • Clear gutters and drains around your property to allow water to flow away from your home.
Pro Tip: Important Documents Keep copies of insurance policies, identification, bank records, and medical information in a secure, waterproof location.

During a Flood

When flooding occurs, act quickly and stay calm. Your decisions matter.

  • Move to higher ground immediately - go to the highest level of your home or a neighbor's if needed.
  • Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters - just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down, and 12 inches can sweep a car away.
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts - TV, radio, or NOAA Weather Radio for updates and instructions.
  • Turn off utilities if instructed and if you can do so safely without standing in water.
  • Avoid bridges over fast-moving water - find alternate routes or wait for rescue if trapped.
  • Stay inside until officially declared safe - even if the rain stops, flooding can continue for hours.
WARNING: Never Drive Through Flood Water Most flood deaths occur in vehicles. If your car stalls in rising water, abandon it immediately and move to higher ground.
!

After a Flood

The danger isn't over when the water recedes. Follow these steps for safe recovery.

  • Return home only when authorities say it's safe - check for structural damage before entering.
  • Wear protective gear including boots, gloves, and masks when cleaning - flood water may contain sewage and chemicals.
  • Document all damage with photos and videos before starting cleanup for insurance purposes.
  • Discard food and water that may have come into contact with flood water - when in doubt, throw it out.
  • Ventilate and dry your home - open doors and windows, use fans and dehumidifiers to prevent mold.
  • Check for gas leaks - if you smell gas or hear hissing, leave immediately and call the gas company.
  • Contact your insurance company as soon as possible to start the claims process.
  • Seek medical attention for any injuries, and watch for signs of infection or illness.
Recovery Tip: Watch for Mold Mold can begin growing within 24-48 hours. Dry out your home quickly and clean all surfaces with a bleach solution (1 cup bleach per gallon of water).
