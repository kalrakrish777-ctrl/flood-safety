Before a Flood

Preparation is your best defense. Take these steps before flooding occurs to protect your family and property.

  • 01
    Build an Emergency Kit Water (1 gallon per person per day), non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, and medications.
  • 02
    Create a Family Communication Plan Establish meeting points and emergency contacts. Write them down and share with all family members.
  • 03
    Know Your Evacuation Routes Identify high ground locations and practice reaching them from your home before an emergency.
  • 04
    Protect Important Documents Store copies of insurance policies, identification, and bank records in waterproof bags or cloud storage.
  • 05
    Elevate Utilities Move electrical panels, furnaces, and water heaters to higher floors when possible.
  • 06
    Sign Up for Emergency Alerts Register for local government notification systems to receive early warnings.
  • 07
    Clear Gutters and Drains Remove debris from around your property to allow water to flow away from your home.
Pro Tip Keep copies of medical records, insurance policies, and identification in a secure, waterproof location. Digital copies in cloud storage are also recommended.

During a Flood

When flooding occurs, act quickly and stay calm. Your decisions in these moments can save lives.

  • 01
    Move to Higher Ground Go to the highest level of your home or a neighbor's immediately. Do not wait for instructions.
  • 02
    Never Enter Flood Waters Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down. 12 inches can sweep a car away. Stay out of all standing water.
  • 03
    Listen to Emergency Broadcasts Monitor TV, radio, or NOAA Weather Radio for updates and official instructions.
  • 04
    Turn Off Utilities If instructed and safe to do so, turn off electricity, gas, and water at the main switches.
  • 05
    Avoid Bridges Over Water They can collapse from water force. Find alternate routes or wait for rescue if trapped.
  • 06
    Stay Put Until Declared Safe Even if rain stops, flooding can continue for hours. Wait for official all-clear.
Warning Most flood deaths occur in vehicles. If your car stalls in rising water, abandon it immediately and move to higher ground.

After a Flood

The danger isn't over when the water recedes. Follow these steps for safe recovery.

  • 01
    Wait for Official Clearance Return home only when authorities say it's safe. Check for structural damage before entering.
  • 02
    Wear Protective Gear Boots, gloves, and masks are essential. Flood water contains sewage and chemicals.
  • 03
    Document Everything Take photos and videos of all damage before cleanup. This is critical for insurance claims.
  • 04
    Discard Contaminated Food Throw away any food or water that may have contacted flood water. When in doubt, throw it out.
  • 05
    Dry Your Home Quickly Open doors and windows. Use fans and dehumidifiers. Mold grows within 24-48 hours.
  • 06
    Check for Gas Leaks If you smell gas or hear hissing, leave immediately. Call the gas company from outside.
  • 07
    Contact Your Insurance File a claim as soon as possible. Document all communications and keep receipts.
  • 08
    Seek Medical Attention Get help for injuries. Watch for signs of infection or illness in the following weeks.
Recovery Tip Mix 1 cup of bleach per gallon of water to clean mold-prone surfaces. Dry everything within 24-48 hours.
